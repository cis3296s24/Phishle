What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime tactic employed by individuals or groups to trick users into exposing sensitive information such as passwords, bank accounts, social security numbers, credit card numbers, or personal identification details.

Typically initiated via email, phishing attempts often impersonate entities like banks, online retailers, or government agencies. These fraudulent messages appear legitimate and prompt recipients to click on links, download attachments, or enter confidential data on fake websites designed to resemble real ones.

Once obtained, the stolen information can be used for identity theft, financial fraud, or other malicious purposes. Phishing attacks exploit human vulnerabilities rather than technical weaknesses, making awareness and caution essential defenses against falling victim to such scams.

Tips For How to Avoid and Defend Yourself from Phishing Scams

  • Avoid clicking on external links in emails unless you are certain about the contents
  • Secure accounts with multi-factor authentication
  • Verify incoming emails to confirm their authenticity, ensuring they originate from legitimate sources.
  • If the email is from a large company, you can directly call the company to confirm
  • Never give your password or sensitive account information out
  • Regularly check logins and activity on accounts to make sure you have not become a victim of phishing
  • If you do become a victim, contact the related companies immediately and have them lock your account

How to Detect Phishing Scams

  • Will attempt to get you to input information and solicit personal details
  • Will lure you with something enticing such as a coupon or refund for engagement
  • Will impersonate reputable companies, involving fabricated account issues to prompt actions
  • Will sometimes contain grammatical errors or spelling mistakes in the email, indicating fraud
  • Will use emails that mimic real companies but have subtle variations
